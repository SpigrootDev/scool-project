Jazz

Background

Jazz emerged in the United States in the early 20th century, especially in African-American communities. It was more than just a style of music - it was an expression of freedom, improvisation and cultural exchange.

Jazz and World War II

Ban in Nazi Germany: The National Socialists considered jazz "degenerate music" because it was associated with African-American culture. Nevertheless, young people developed secret jazz clubs to rebel against the regime. Resistance and hope: Jazz stations such as "BBC" and "Radio Free Europe" broadcast jazz music to Germany to strengthen the spirit of resistance.

Important artists:

  • Louis Armstrong
  • Duke Ellington
  • Billie Holiday
